Portfolio Overview

The Idea Behind This Portfolio

KKP Wealth V2 is a growth-oriented institutional managed portfolio designed for high-net-worth THB investors seeking higher long-term returns. It removes the Expanded portfolio's large cash (27%) and gold (14%) positions, replacing them with increased equity exposure via ACWI passive, Capital Group active management, doubled thematic satellite positions, and a standalone PIMCO global flexible bond allocation. The result is a portfolio targeting ~8.5–9.5% expected return with higher volatility.

Fixed Income (11.82%)
Standalone PIMCO GIS Global Bond — active flexible bond management providing diversified fixed income exposure with ability to go anywhere in global bond markets.
Equity (2.18%)
Local Thai equity via VAYU1 for direct THB-denominated stock market exposure.
Mandate (36.00%)
KKP Balanced Standard (31% offshore) and KKP Aggressive THB (5% onshore) — managed mandates providing diversified global exposure. Onshore switched from Moderate to Aggressive.
Alternative (20.00%)
Lazard Global Infrastructure (8%), Fund of Hedge Funds (6%), and KKR K-PRIME Private Equity (6%) — no gold, focused on real assets and illiquidity premium.
Satellite (30.00%)
Thematic equity (10%), ACWI Passive (10%), Capital Group Active (8%), and DO Autocallable Notes (2%) — dramatically expanded from 5% to 30%.

V2 achieves higher expected returns through aggressive equity allocation (~43%), elimination of cash/gold buffers, and expanded satellite positions. This comes with significantly higher drawdown risk in adverse scenarios.

What V2 Does Well

Verdict

V2 is a fundamentally different portfolio from the Expanded version. It trades capital preservation and crisis resilience for growth potential. The expected return rises from ~5–6% to ~8.5–9.5%, but maximum drawdown risk roughly doubles. V2 is appropriate for investors with a 10+ year horizon who can tolerate 20–30% drawdowns without changing their plan. The zero gold and zero cash positions are the most aggressive design choices — in a prolonged bear market or geopolitical crisis, there is no buffer to cushion the fall or provide opportunistic rebalancing capital.
